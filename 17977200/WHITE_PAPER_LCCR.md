# The Law of Coherent Continuity of Reality

DOI (Zenodo): 10.5281/zenodo.17977201

## Abstract
This paper introduces the Law of Coherent Continuity of Reality (LCCR), a phenomenological and ontological law stating that the real future of any system is uniquely determined by the requirement of preserving the coherence of its past. The law does not describe dynamics, decision-making, or probabilistic evolution. Instead, it formalizes a selection constraint on what can physically exist in time. While multiple futures may be conceivable, only one future can be actualized: the one that prolongs the past without contradiction in terms of coherence phenomenale. We show that this principle reconciles determinism and unpredictability by distinguishing ontological determinism from epistemic indeterminacy. The law integrates results from physics of time, neuroscience of consciousness delay, and information theory, and is compatible with the ZORAN framework through the metric of coherence phenomenale Cφ.

## 1. Introduction
The question of whether the future is deterministic has historically been framed as a conflict between physical causality and subjective experience. Classical determinism asserts that the future follows necessarily from the past, while modern approaches introduce probabilistic, cognitive, or emergent indeterminacy.
