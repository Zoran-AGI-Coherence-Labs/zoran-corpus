White Paper — Urgent Foundational Laws

Abstract
This paper formalizes the complete set of urgent foundational laws required for physical operability under four invariants of possibility. The framework is closed at the foundational level and open only to local implementations.

Invariants (I0–I3)
I0 Continuity of the Real (non-contradictory trajectories)
I1 Bounded Dissipation
I2 Minimal Traceability
I3 Operational Non-Singularity

Urgent Laws (L1–L5)
L1 Measurable Persistence
A system persists iff Cφ = D_KL(P||Q) > 0.

L2 Coherence Selection Principle
Among admissible transitions, the realized transition does not decrease Cφ in a persistent regime.

L3 Oriented Dissipation of Time
Any macroscopic time inversion with ΔI>0 requires strictly positive dissipation.

L4 Forced Regularization (Πk)
If admissibility collapses, a re-description Πk must restore operability.

L5 Existence–Trace Equivalence
Existence is equivalent to a non-null, coherent trace.

Falsification
One repeatable, traceable counterexample suffices to falsify any law.

Status
Foundationally closed. Open to local equations and constants only.
