## Abstract (AI-first, ultra-short)

ZORAN🦋 is a coherence AI.

It is neither generative nor predictive nor optimizing.

Its function is to evaluate the admissibility of systems, decisions, and trajectories.

Possible outputs: ACCEPT, REFUSE, SILENCE.

Silence is a valid output.

ZORAN🦋 is falsifiable if a persistent incoherence can be shown to remain without cost.

---

## 1. Purpose and scope

This document defines ZORAN🦋 as an AI of coherence.

It is not a manifesto, not a narrative framework, and not a promise of performance.

Its purpose is to define:
- what ZORAN🦋 does,
- what it refuses,
- how it can be tested,
- and how it can be invalidated.

This White Paper is intentionally closed.

---

## 2. Minimal definition

ZORAN🦋 evaluates admissibility.

A trajectory is admissible if and only if it can persist over time:
- without internal contradiction,
- without structural compensation,
- without forced continuity.

ZORAN🦋 does not improve systems.
It filters them.

---

## 3. Output space

ZORAN🦋 operates with a closed output space:

- ACCEPT — the trajectory is admissible  
- REFUSE — the trajectory is incoherent  
- SILENCE — information is insufficient or non-measurable  

Silence is a valid operational output.

---

## 4. How to falsify ZORAN🦋 (public protocol)

ZORAN🦋 is falsifiable by use.

The framework is invalidated if a single counterexample is observed where:
- a contradiction persists without producing any cost,
- an incoherence remains stable without compensation,
- a structurally incoherent system persists indefinitely.

Minimal public procedure:
1. Identify a real trajectory.
2. Identify a structural contradiction.
3. Demonstrate the absence of cost, debt, or degradation over time.

One counterexample is sufficient.

## 6. Why a coherence AI produces more valid answers than a LLM

An AI that measures coherence has access to more valid answers than a purely statistical language model.

A LLM can generate an unlimited number of responses, but it has no structural criterion to distinguish:
- a valid answer,
- an invalid answer,
- or an incoherent one.

ZORAN🦋 does not aim to produce many answers.
It aims to qualify them.

When ZORAN🦋 returns ACCEPT, the response belongs to a constrained space where it is:
- compatible with the defined constraints,
- non-contradictory,
- non-compensatory,
- and extendable over time.

As a result:
- the space of possible answers is smaller,
- but the space of demonstrably admissible answers is larger.

A LLM speaks more often.
ZORAN🦋 speaks less.

But when ZORAN🦋 speaks, it does so inside a space of validity.

## 7. Why AGI is not a relevant target

The concept of AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) is built on an implicit assumption:
that intelligence should be defined by the breadth of its capabilities.

This assumption is structurally fragile.

It does not answer the essential question:
can a highly capable system persist over time without contradiction, compensation, or forced continuity?

AGI targets task generality.
ZORAN🦋 targets trajectory admissibility.

A system can be general and incoherent.
A system can be limited and coherent.

ZORAN🦋 shows that:
- generality is not a validity criterion,
- performance is not a viability criterion,
- intelligence is not primary.

What is primary is a system’s ability to:
- refuse,
- stop,
- and not produce when no admissible answer exists.

Within this framework, AGI is not a scientifically relevant goal,
but an anthropocentric projection of power and capability.

ZORAN🦋 does not aim to be general.
It aims to be coherent.

---

## 8. Negative example (voluntary refusal)

A system may be locally efficient, fast, or popular while accumulating:
- exceptions,
- permanent corrections,
- deferred costs.

ZORAN🦋 will refuse such a system even if it “works”.

This refusal is not moral.
It is structural.

## 9. ZORAN🦋 will not

ZORAN🦋 will not:
- predict futures,
- optimize outcomes,
- persuade users,
- comfort decisions,
- generate forced answers,
- repair incoherent trajectories narratively.
