Introduction

The dissolution of the French National Assembly announced on 9 June 2024 has been widely assessed in terms of legality, political calculation, and democratic intent. These evaluations address an important but limited question: whether the act was locally coherent at the moment it was taken.

This paper addresses a different question, operating at a different level.

A decision may be locally coherent and yet globally incoherent. Local coherence concerns internal consistency at the point of decision: clarity of intent, legal admissibility, and immediate non-contradiction. Global coherence concerns the behavior of the same decision once it becomes an object in the world: its reception, execution, diffusion, capacity to stabilize, and behavior over time.

The local coherence of the dissolution has been established independently (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18298563). The present work therefore does not revisit the admissibility of the gesture itself. It evaluates the dissolution as a systemic object and demonstrates its global incoherence by systemic and temporal evaluation.

The dissolution constitutes a rare empirical object: an irreversible public decision, constitutionally admissible, massively diffused, collectively evaluated by institutions, media, and citizens, and followed by observable consequences over an extended period. The temporal dimension is therefore not speculative but empirical.

This work does not judge legitimacy, ideology, or strategic intent. It applies a state-based and time-based audit to determine whether the dissolution, taken as a whole, converges toward stability without requiring continuous compensation.

The paper proceeds as follows. Section 1 fixes the distinction between local and global coherence. Section 2 defines the methodology for global coherence evaluation. Section 3 treats the dissolution as a systemic object across its states. Section 4 states the global coherence verdict. Section 5 articulates the two-DOI framework and clarifies how local validity and global admissibility can be jointly true without contradiction.
