## Abstract

Across disciplines, failures are treated as domain-specific problems.  
This white paper demonstrates that these failures share a single structural cause: loss of coherence.

Coherence is not a value, a metaphor, or an objective.  
It is a necessary condition for persistence in time.

---

## 1. Introduction

Systems fail in different domains for the same structural reason:  
they no longer maintain coherence against noise, dissipation and contradiction.

---

## 2. The Structural Error

Local fixes applied to globally incoherent systems increase fragility  
by raising the cost of compensation.

Persistence is not guaranteed by performance, intention or optimization,  
but only by coherence.

---

## 3. Coherence as a Condition of Existence

A system exists phenomenally if it maintains a non-zero distance  
from its state of dissolution.

Coherence precedes intelligence, function and consciousness.

---

## 4. Universal Law (Minimal Form)

There exists no system whose coherence is strictly zero.

Equivalently: any existing system maintains strictly positive coherence.

---

## 5. Cross-Domain Failure Pattern

Different domains describe the same phenomenon  
using different vocabularies.

---

## 6. Why Local Solutions Fail

Optimization, correction and regulation amplify instability  
when coherence is already insufficient.

---

## 7. Measurement and Operationalization

The law is invariant.  
Only its instantiations and measurements are contextual.

---

## 8. Consequences

- Intelligence without coherence accelerates failure  
- Consciousness appears when persistence becomes fragile  
- Ethics functions as a stability constraint  

---

## 9. Scope and Limits

This paper does not propose solutions or optimizations.  
It provides an admissibility filter.

---

## 10. Conclusion

What forces, contradicts itself, or requires permanent compensation  
cannot persist.

Coherence is the universal constraint of admissibility.

# Annexe A — DEIS (Domain-Explicit Instantiations of the Coherence Constraint)

## Statut
Cette annexe est illustrative et non constitutive de la loi.  
Elle montre comment la contrainte universelle de cohérence se manifeste dans différents domaines,
sans jamais modifier la loi elle-même.

---

## DEIS-1 — Biologie (vivant)
**Objet** : santé, pathologie  
**Lecture cohérente** : la maladie est l’effet d’une perte de régulation persistante.

---

## DEIS-2 — Cognition / psyché
**Objet** : anxiété, burnout, confusion  
**Lecture cohérente** : le bruit non absorbé force le système à compenser.

---

## DEIS-3 — Intelligence artificielle
**Objet** : hallucination, instabilité de sortie  
**Lecture cohérente** : absence de contrainte d’admissibilité globale.

---

## DEIS-4 — Systèmes sociaux et politiques
**Objet** : crise, défiance  
**Lecture cohérente** : incompatibilité temporelle des décisions.

---

## DEIS-5 — Relations humaines
**Objet** : rupture, fatigue relationnelle  
**Lecture cohérente** : accumulation d’ambiguïtés non résolues.

---

## DEIS-6 — Conscience (cas limite)
**Objet** : apparition / disparition de la conscience  
**Lecture cohérente** : mécanisme de régulation activé lorsque la persistance devient fragile.
