Preface — Local and Global Coherence

A decision can be coherent locally and incoherent globally at the same time.

Local coherence evaluates validity at the point of decision. Global coherence evaluates maintainability over time. Confusing them leads to the persistent defense of locally valid decisions that generate systemic instability.

This paper evaluates the dissolution at the global level and complements the local coherence evaluation rather than contradicting it.
