Executive Summary

This white paper evaluates the dissolution of the French National Assembly announced on 9 June 2024 using a two-level coherence framework.

Local coherence evaluates admissibility at the point of action (clarity, legal admissibility, immediate non-contradiction). The local coherence of the act has been established independently (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18298563).

Global coherence evaluates admissibility over time (reception, execution, diffusion, corrective load, and convergence toward stability). Applying this criterion to the dissolution yields a clear result: corrective pressure did not diminish and no convergence toward stability was observed. The trajectory required sustained compensation.

Therefore, the dissolution is locally coherent but globally incoherent. The two statements are jointly true because they refer to different levels of evaluation.
