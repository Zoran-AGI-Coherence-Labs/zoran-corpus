# 🧠 Operational Consciousness as a Global Rescue Controller
## End-to-End Demonstration and Applications
**DOI:** 10.5281/zenodo.18359622  
**Link:** https://zenodo.org/records/18359622

## Abstract

This white paper proposes an **operational** definition of consciousness as an **intermittent, costly global control regime**, activated when local regulatory mechanisms fail to preserve a system’s coherence. Using public neurophysiological contexts (sleep, anesthesia, cortical perturbation, disorders of consciousness), we define an **instrumented** framework linking observables, metrics, dynamics, decisions, and applications. The framework is **cross-domain**, **falsifiable**, and includes an explicit **structural indeterminacy output** as a non-absorbing condition of testability. Within this framework, **coherence** is the **transversal evaluation criterion** for system trajectories, while **consciousness** is treated as an **operational mechanism** that may be mobilized when that criterion is threatened.

---

## 1. Epistemic positioning

This work does not address subjective experience, qualia, or ontology. It determines the instrumental conditions under which a system mobilizes global control to preserve an admissible trajectory. Any claim that cannot be tested, rejected, or declared indeterminate is out of scope.

---

## 2. Structural space and dynamic regime

Metric validity is declared within an explicit structural space: systems that are

- open,
- non-stationary,
- partially observable,
- causally perturbable,
- with non-fixed trajectories a priori.

Outside this space (closed, frozen, or non-perturbable systems), metrics are not guaranteed nor interpretable. The framework is therefore conditional by construction, not universal by postulate.

---

## 2bis. Coherence as criterion, consciousness as mechanism

This framework separates an evaluation criterion from an operational mechanism.

Coherence is the criterion by which a dynamic trajectory is evaluated as admissible, stable, or dissolving. It is assessed independently of any global control mechanism and does not require subjective assumptions.

Consciousness is not the criterion. It is an operational mechanism that may be activated when coherence is threatened and local regulation is insufficient. Its role is functional: arbitrate, reparameterize, or suspend action to attempt to restore an admissible trajectory.

This hierarchy prevents circularity: coherence is neither defined by consciousness nor used to calibrate its own metrics.

---

## 3. Independent observables

Upstream observables are defined prior to the framework:

- raw neurophysiological signals (EEG, fMRI, TMS-EEG),
- connectivity structures,
- temporal dynamics (variability, transitions, latency),
- causal perturbation responses.

No observable is defined from coherence itself.

---

## 4. Metrics and anti-circularity

Metrics are explicit mathematical functions of observables (complexity, integration, stability, propagation, cost). They are defined a priori, remain computable even if the central hypothesis is false, and are not tuned case-by-case.

Coherence is not used to select observables, tune metrics, or set thresholds. It is inferred a posteriori when tests hold, or rejected when tests fail.

---

## 5. Decision pipeline and indeterminacy

Pipeline: observables → metrics → dynamics → decision.

When structural conditions fail (invariance rupture, perturbational inconsistency, cross-regime transfer failure), the pipeline outputs explicit indeterminacy. This is a valid output and the non-absorbing falsifiability criterion (refusal to conclude rather than reparameterization).

---

## 6. Falsifiable hypotheses and rejection criteria

All hypotheses admit observable negations.

- H1 Intermittence: global control appears preferentially at critical transitions.
- H2 Cost: global control entails measurable cost.
- H3 Transversality: signatures are conserved across domains.
- H4 Perturbation: perturbation adds causal discrimination.
- H5 Boundaries: specific signatures appear at stability boundaries.
- H6 Reportability: global control may persist without explicit report.

The framework is rejected if any of the following is reproducibly observed:

1. results survive label shuffles or negative controls,
2. ordinal relations break under perturbation,
3. cross-regime transfer fails systematically,
4. indeterminacy cannot be produced as a formal output,
5. cost never differentiates regimes when global control is supposed active.

---

## 7. Applications

Applications follow directly from the pipeline:

- anesthesia: regime transition detection,
- disorders of consciousness: longitudinal trajectories,
- sleep: functional boundary mapping,
- artificial systems: global fallback controller without anthropomorphism.

Outputs are decisions or refusals to decide, never experiential interpretations.

---

## 8. Closure

Coherence is not a property to explain here, but a criterion that decides; consciousness is not the yardstick, but a subordinate functional mechanism activated when local regulation fails to preserve an admissible trajectory.

Any discussion that does not engage an explicit structural space, independent observables, a priori metrics, and formal indeterminacy is out of scope.
